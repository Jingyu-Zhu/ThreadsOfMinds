What is Alzheimer’s Disease?

There are nearly one million Canadians who live with dementia today. Alzheimer’s is the most common type, gradually affecting thinking, behaviour, and memory—often to the point where daily tasks become impossible without support.

While there is currently no cure, researchers point to a combination of genetics, environment, and lifestyle. The good news is that informed choices can reduce risk and slow progression. Explore our resource library to learn the warning signs, treatment options, and how to advocate for loved ones.

Core Steps Against Alzheimer’s Disease

Start with awareness, follow with daily prevention, and keep the conversation going in your community.

Be Informed

Recognize early warning signs like unusual memory loss, confusion with time and place, and difficulty speaking. Timely screening can lead to earlier access to care.

Preventative Measures

Nutritious meals, quality sleep, stress management, and mental stimulation all contribute to a healthier brain. Small habits now can preserve memories later.

Spread Awareness
